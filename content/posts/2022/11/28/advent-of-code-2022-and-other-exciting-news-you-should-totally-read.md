Last year was SO EXHAUSTING.

But I finished. A couple days I got some help from other solvers, but I eventually wrote programs to solve every day, two parts. I learned a heck of a lot that I didn't know about the Python programming language, algorithms, data structures, and the behavior of submarines in the festive frozen waters beneath the North Pole.

I documented the whole thing on Twitter but, well, Twitter doesn't like me anymore, so I'm just going to post each day's results here.

And just to be sure that I don't get those solutions done too quickly, I will solve them in Python first -- just to get them done and on the leader board -- and then see how I can get them into PICO-8.
