I asked OpenAI's GPT-3 to generate 31 different videogame ideas, one for each day of Blaugust, each dealing in some way with that particular day of the month. Here's its idea for August 5th: "Steve, the Fifth Horseman of the Apocalypse".

This is a game where you play as Steve, the fifth horseman of the Apocalypse, who was left behind when the other four horsemen rode off to bring about the end of the world. With only your trusty steed and a few weapons, you must now ride across the country, fighting off hordes of zombies and other monsters that have taken over the world.

Together with your trusty steed, "Buck", this 2D platformer/shooter will have you galloping through levels, blasting zombies with your shotgun or mowing them down with your trusty chainsaw. You'll also be able to find and use a variety of other weapons, including a machine gun, a rocket launcher, and even a flamethrower. Along the way you'll find power-ups that will help you survive, as well as new mounts that will let you travel even faster.

Each chapter ends with a battle against one of Steve's brothers. The fight against his brother, Pestilence, is a particularly challenging one, as you must face off against a giant, mutated zombie. War, on the other hand, will have you riding into battle against an army of zombies, while Famine will have you trying to survive in a post-apocalyptic wasteland.

Once you have defeated all your brothers, you'll be able to ride into the sunset and claim your rightful place as the *only *horseman of the Apocalypse.

**Gameplay**

"Steve: The Fifth Horseman" resembles many of the most famous 2D platformers, like "Super Mario World". However, it sets itself apart with its focus on combat. The majority of the game will have you fighting off hordes of zombies, using a variety of weapons. While the game is not particularly difficult, it can be challenging at times. The final battle against Pestilence, in particular, is quite difficult. However, the game is forgiving and allows you to continue from checkpoints if you die.

**Graphics and Sound**

The graphics in "Steve: The Fifth Horseman" are colorful and detailed. The 2D sprites are nicely animated and the environments are well-designed. The game's aesthetic is reminiscent of a Saturday morning cartoon, which fits well with its light-hearted tone. The game's sound design is also quite good. The soundtrack consists of upbeat rock tunes that fit the game's tone perfectly. The sound effects are also well done and add to the game's overall atmosphere.

**DLC**

The game includes a number of DLC packs, which add new mounts, weapons, and levels. The "Buck's Wild" DLC pack, for example, adds a new mount, as well as a new set of levels set in the Wild West. The "Pestilence's Revenge" DLC pack adds a new boss fight against Pestilence, as well as a new set of levels set in a post-apocalyptic wasteland.

**Conclusion**

"Steve: The Fifth Horseman" is a fun and challenging 2D platformer/shooter that is perfect for fans of the genre. The game's colorful graphics and sound design are top-notch, and its focus on combat is sure to please fans of action games.
