It might go a little like this. But what if the Avengers were talking about it, or it was being reviewed by Siskel & Ebert? Keep reading!

Dear Abby,

I have been playing Star Ocean: The Divine Force, and I have a problem. I’m in love with a robot.

I know, I know. I’m not supposed to fall in love with robots. I’m supposed to fall in love with a man. A man who is a space pirate and who crashed his escape pod at my feet.

This robot, that I’m in love with, is also a man. I know, because he told me so. But he’s a robot. I can’t love him. I’m supposed to fall in love with Raymond, the space pirate.

But I can’t. I try, but I can’t.

Abby, I love this robot. I don’t know why. He’s a robot. But he makes my heart melt and my face smile, like a good man is supposed to do. And he has a very nice smile.

I know, I know. He’s a robot. But I love him.

I wish I could be with him. But I can’t. I’m the daughter of the king of the Humans, and he’s a robot. I’m not supposed to fall in love with robots.

But I do. I love him, Abby. What am I supposed to do?

Desperate in Elencia

Dear Desperate in Elencia,

I'm sorry to hear that you are in love with a robot. While it is understandable that you are drawn to him because he makes you feel happy and he has a nice smile, you have to remember that he is not a real man. He is a machine and, as such, cannot give you the same type of love and companionship that you would experience with a human being.

If you truly want to be happy, you need to find a way to move on from this robot and focus your attentions on finding a real man to love. I know it will be difficult, but if you can do it, you will be much better off in the long run.
